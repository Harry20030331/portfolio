  2. Multi-agent long-horizon quadrupedal pushing

    Feb. 2024 – Oct. 2024 · Pittsburgh, USA Advised by Prof. Ding Zhao (CMU) and Prof. Jie Tan (Google DeepMind)

    Proposed and developed a three-level hierarchical MARL framework for long-horizon, collaborative quadrupedal pushing in obstacle-cluttered environments.

    Benchmarked against baselines: +36.0% success and −24.5% completion time vs. the best baseline. Deployed on real quadrupeds for Push-T and Push-Cuboid over 10–20 m (~0.5 m/s) with obstacle-aware coordinated whole-body motion.

    Published at IEEE ICRA 2025.

    Paper · Website · Code

  3. Collaborative planar pushing in cluttered scenes

    Sept. 2023 – May. 2024 · Beijing, China Advised by Prof. Meng Guo, Peking University

    Addressed collaborative planar pushing of polytopic objects in obstacle-cluttered scenes via hybrid optimization over contact modes and pushing forces, paired with a hierarchical hybrid search that decomposes paths and selects feasible parameterized modes.

    Applied nonlinear MPC on each mobile robot to track desired pushing velocities online. Validated in high-fidelity simulation and hardware and demonstrated robustness to motion and actuation uncertainty.

    Published at Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS) 2024.
